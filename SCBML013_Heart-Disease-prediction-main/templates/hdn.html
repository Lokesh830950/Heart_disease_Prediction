HEART DISEASE PREVENTIONS

DIET

Having a healthy diet is one of the most important precautions to take in order to prevent heart disease. Some key dietary guidelines include:

  • Choose lean proteins like fish, poultry, and legumes instead of red meat
  • Limit intake of processed and packaged foods
  • Choose unsaturated fats like olive oil and avocados instead of saturated and trans fats
  • Choosing whole grains instead of refined grains
  • Avoid sugary drinks like soda and energy drinks
  • Incorporate heart-healthy foods like nuts, seeds, and fatty fish into your diet



EXERCISE

Regular exercise is also important for preventing heart disease.

  • Aim for a combination of moderate-intensity and vigorous-intensity aerobic exercises
  • Incorporate physical activity into your daily routine, such as taking the stairs instead of the elevator or going for a walk during your lunch break
  • Join a fitness class or sports team to stay motivated and accountable
  • Gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts over time
  • Mix up your routine with different types of exercises, such as swimming or cycling in addition to walking or running



PREVENTIONS

There are also some important prevetnions to take in order to prevent heart disease:

  • Avoid or limit exposure to air pollution
  • Practice good oral hygiene and visit the dentist regularly
  • Avoid or limit consumption of foods high in sodium and added sugars
  • Learn to manage stress through relaxation techniques or talking to a therapist
  • Wear sunscreen to protect your skin from harmful UV rays



TO CARE

Regular care over your body

  • Know your family history of heart disease and discuss it with your doctor
  • Stay up to date on recommended heart disease screenings and tests
  • Take medications as prescribed and report any side effects to your doctor
  • Learn to recognize the signs and symptoms of a heart attack, such as chest pain or discomfort, shortness of breath, and lightheadedness
  • Follow a healthy lifestyle even after a heart disease diagnosis or treatment.
